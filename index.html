What's shaping your wardrobe?

Since 2022, fashion and culture critics have been taking to the internet to proclaim the death of personal style at the hands of social media algorithms and fast fashion companies. So where's your style coming from?

Juliana George and Shreya Thalvayapati

In the age of fast-moving digital trends, it is easier to buy into viral moments but harder to cultivate a wardrobe that feels authentic. The rise of TikTok Shop has accelerated the trend cycle — one week, it's all about clean-girl minimalism; the next, it's coquette bows and indie sleaze. While some sources of inspiration encourage creativity and longevity, others fuel impulsive, short-lived purchases.

With fashion shifting at breakneck speed, where are people actually finding their inspiration? And in a world of algorithm-driven style, is it still possible to develop a look that's truly your own? Let's take a closer look at how the trend cycle shapes our choices and what it really means to dress with intention in the digital age.

You might be a victim of microtrends!

In the second half of 2023, this cropped "Brasil" baby tee was everywhere. A staple of the soccer-inspired "blokecore" aesthetic that started trending in 2022, vintage jerseys have been selling for twice their value on reseller sites like Depop for a few years now. And after celebrities like Hailey Bieber, SZA, and Kali Uchis all posted photos wearing similar Brasil jerseys, it was only a matter of time before fast fashion retailers like Shein started mass-manufacturing a cheap alternative.

If they're your style, a jersey is a good staple to have that you can wear a lot of different ways. But if you're just buying one to follow a trend, you're less likely to invest in a piece that won't end up in a landfill after five or so wears. When drawing inspiration from social media trends, try to keep in mind the longevity of a piece and whether you'll still like it when the trend is through.

What does this all mean?

In a world where trends are dictated by algorithms and the latest viral moment, not every source of inspiration encourages mindful consumption—some lead to fleeting purchases that don't integrate well into a long-term wardrobe. The key to sustainable and intentional fashion isn't about chasing every new aesthetic. Whether your style influences come from the streets, social media, runways, or film, what matters most is finding inspiration that truly resonates with you. Because at the end of the day, the best style isn't just trendy—it's timeless.
